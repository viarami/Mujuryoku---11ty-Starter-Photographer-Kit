A strong wedding gallery depends on a realistic timeline. Most missed photos are not technical mistakes, they are scheduling issues.

## Build in margin

Add short buffers between every major block:

- Getting ready
- First look
- Ceremony
- Family portraits
- Reception entrances

Even 10-15 minute buffers prevent cascading delays.

## Priority photo blocks

### Getting ready
Capture details early (dress, rings, invitations) before rooms become busy.

### Family formals
Prepare a written group list in advance and assign one person to gather each group.

### Couple portraits
Protect at least one dedicated block with calm pacing. Golden hour is ideal when possible.

### Reception moments
Coordinate with the DJ or planner for exact timings of speeches, first dance, and cake cutting.

## Final tip

Share the final timeline with all vendors at least 48 hours before the event. Alignment across teams is the easiest way to improve your photo results.
